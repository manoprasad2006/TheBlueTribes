The Kurumba Tribe

A living legacy of art, nature, and ancestral wisdom

Guardians of the Nilgiris

The Kurumbas are one of the most ancient indigenous tribes of the Nilgiri hills in Southern India. Traditionally known as food gatherers and hunters, they share a deep, spiritual bond with the forests they inhabit.

For centuries, the Kurumbas have expressed their worldview through distinctive painting styles, depicting festivals, honey gathering, and rituals that honor nature's rhythms. However, the number of traditional artists is decreasing, with fewer members of the younger generation taking up the mantle. This platform aims to bridge the gap, treating these products not just as artifacts, but as "Premium Narratives" that support livelihoods.

Art & Cultural Identity

Simple strokes, profound meanings. The Kurumba art style is characterized by geometric figures and earth-toned palettes, telling stories of a life intertwined with the wild.

Visual Syntax

Art that adheres to a strict cultural grammar, distinguishing between 'Open' motifs shared for storytelling and 'Sacred' symbols reserved exclusively for ritual use.

Sacred Groves

Every artwork uses natural pigments to honor the forest. The creation process itself is a form of stewardship, aligned with the rhythms of the harvest and the monsoon.

Living Symbolism

Geometric simplicity with profound meaning. Vertical lines and circles are not just shapes but a language of memory, identity, and community unity.

The Artist's Legacy

In this digital archive, we honor the individual artists who carry the torch of Kurumba tradition. Their hands weave the stories of the past into the canvas of the present, ensuring that the wisdom of the elders is not lost to time.


"We do not just paint what we see; we paint what we feel, what the forest whispers to us."
